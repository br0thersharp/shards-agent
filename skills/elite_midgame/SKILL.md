# Elite Midgame — Board Control & Tempo (Turns 4-8)

> The midgame is about denying the opponent resources while developing yours. Whoever controls the board at turn 8 wins the game.

## BOARD CONTROL DOCTRINE

### The Rule of 4
Never let your opponent have more than 4 creatures on board. Wide boards snowball — each additional creature multiplies their attack options and overwhelms your removal. If they reach 5+, you're losing the board war.

**Board states and what to do:**
- **You have more creatures:** ATTACK EVERY TURN. Force trades. Your extra creatures mean you come out ahead
- **Board is even:** Play creatures to tip the balance, THEN attack. Removal their best creature first
- **They have more creatures:** REMOVE before developing. Clear their board, then play creatures into an empty/weak board
- **They have 5+ creatures:** EMERGENCY. Logic Bomb + Crippling Despair combo. If you don't have AoE, you're in trouble — block efficiently and pray for a draw

### Board Position = "Center of the Board"
In Shards, board presence IS positional advantage. The player with more Vigilant creatures controls the game because Vigilant creatures attack AND defend. They're the equivalent of controlling the center in chess — flexible, threatening in all directions.

**Priority creatures for board control:**
1. Master Strategist (gives ALL your creatures Vigilant — the ultimate board control card)
2. Elite Enforcer (3/3 Vigilant + draws cards on combat damage)
3. Restoration Engine (3/4 Vigilant + taps enemy creature each upkeep — removes a blocker every turn)
4. Enforcement Sentinel (2/3 Vigilant + taps enemy creature on entry)

## REMOVAL SEQUENCING

Order matters. Play removal in this sequence:

### Before Combat:
1. **Crippling Despair** (2E, -1/-1 all enemy) — shrinks blockers so your creatures survive trades. Also sets up Logic Bomb (creatures that were power 3 become power 2, now targetable)
2. **Logic Bomb** (3E, 2 damage to all enemy creatures power <=2) — ONLY hits small creatures. Combo with Crippling Despair for maximum wipe. Against wide boards of tokens/small creatures, this is devastating

### Targeted Removal (anytime):
3. **Precision Strike** (2E, 3 damage to creature) — bread and butter. Use on:
   - Engine creatures (Brood Mother, Archive Keeper, Scribe) — kill on sight
   - Creatures with power >= 3 that threaten lethal
   - Stealth creatures (can't block them, must remove)
4. **Purge Protocol** (3E, destroy + draw) — premium removal. Save for:
   - Creatures with 4+ defense (Precision Strike can't kill)
   - Eternal Sentinel [4/5], enemy Hive Tyrant, enemy Master Strategist
5. **Isolation Protocol** (1E, exile power <=1) — specifically for:
   - Archive Keepers [1/3] (exile = no recursion, they're gone forever)
   - Tokens (clean removal for 1E)

### Response Removal (opponent's turn):
6. **Counter Protocol** (2E, counter spell unless they pay 2) — save for:
   - Their bombs (Hive Tyrant, Master Strategist, Temporal Lock)
   - Buff spells that would save a creature you're about to kill
   - Do NOT waste on cheap creatures — let those resolve and remove them with Precision Strike

### Face Damage:
7. **Chaos Bolt** (2E, 1d6 to creature or player) — flexible. Use on creatures during midgame, save for face damage in endgame

## TEMPO MANAGEMENT

### Energy Efficiency
- **Never end a turn with unspent energy and playable cards.** Unspent energy = wasted tempo
- **Double-play when possible.** At 6E, play a 4-drop + 2-drop. At 8E, play a 5-drop + 3-drop
- **Resources still matter.** If you draw a resource in midgame, play it if you have spare energy. Extra energy compounds

### Creature Sequencing
Play creatures BEFORE attacking:
- **Hive Tyrant** tokens have Swift — they attack the turn Hive Tyrant enters. Play Hive Tyrant → declare attackers with tokens
- **Enforcement Sentinel** taps an enemy creature on entry — play it to remove a blocker, then attack
- **Master Strategist** gives all creatures Vigilant — play it to let your tapped creatures block next turn

### Attack Calculus
Before declaring attackers, calculate:
1. **Total unblocked damage** = your attack power - their blocker count (each blocker absorbs one attacker)
2. **Trade quality** = will their blockers kill your attackers? If your creatures survive and theirs die, attack. If both die and yours costs less, attack
3. **Vigilant attackers** don't tap — they can attack AND block next turn. Always include them
4. **Leave back a blocker** if opponent has lethal on board and your HP is low. One Vigilant creature can attack AND block

## MIDGAME THREATS TO KILL ON SIGHT

These cards snowball if left alive. Remove immediately regardless of your other plans:
- **Brood Mother** (1/3, creates 1/1 token each end of turn) — exponential threat
- **Archive Keeper** (1/3, recursion engine) — especially vs C faction
- **Scribe** (recursion, pulls from graveyard) — vs C faction
- **Any Stealth creature** — you can't block it, it deals damage every turn
- **Any Drain creature** — 2x life swing per attack (they gain what you lose)
- **Restoration Engine** (enemy's, taps YOUR creature each upkeep)

## TRANSITION TO ENDGAME

You're in endgame when:
- Either player is below 15 HP, OR
- You have lethal on board (total power > their HP + blocker count), OR
- Turn 9+ and you have a clear board advantage

At this point, switch to endgame decision-making (lethal calculation, finisher deployment, closing kills).
